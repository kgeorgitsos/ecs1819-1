Influencers

Josephine Elizabeth "Bet" Ross

Josephine Elizabeth "Bet" Ross was Ella baker's grandmother. Her grandmother had been whipped for refusing to marry a man chosen for her by the slave owner. Ella was inspired by her grandmother's stories about being born into slavery.

Martin Luther King Jr.

Ella Baker is considered the "backbone" of the civil rights movement who influenced other activist. Even though this is true, she was also influenced by activist Martin Luther King Jr. to continue to fight for her rights.

Rosa Parks

Ella Baker worked along with some famous civil rights activist, such as Rosa Parks. Ella and Rosa influenced each other by showing the strenth of female civil rights activist.

Why She's Famous

People know Ella Baker as the "backbone" of the civil rights movement. She played a major role in the three most influential civil rights groups, the National Association or the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP), the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC) and Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC) While other activist were more main stream, Baker traveled from small towns to small towns and tried to convince "ordinary black citizens" to fight for basic human rights.
